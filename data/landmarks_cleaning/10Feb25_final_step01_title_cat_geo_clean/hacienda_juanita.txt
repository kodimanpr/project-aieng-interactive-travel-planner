The  design is based on typical Puerto Rican culture , and was commissioned by the wife of a Spanish official.  Coffee production at the hacienda declined from the 1960s.  \\n\nHotel\nFrom 1976 it was converted, with government assistance,  into one of Puerto Rico\\'s earliest paradores , and ran, administered by the Puerto Rico Tourism Company , as a 21-room agro- hotel . At 1,600 ft (490 m) above sea level, visitors could enjoy the view and the peacefulness of the Puerto Rican mountains.  The parador passed to new private owners in 2005 but closed in 2011.
